# Note - Executive Thought Leadership Generation Principles

## Executive Summary
- Strong executive thought leadership should be generated through multiple passes and critique loops, not one-shot drafting.
- The narrative arc should be right before prose is polished.
- The writing should be clear, commercial, and useful to senior readers.
- Important claims should be supported with targeted research and proof points.

## 1. Build a Multi-Step Editorial System
- Strong executive writing should not come from a single drafting pass.
- The process should include:
  - multiple rounds of refinement
  - validation and critique between passes
  - explicit rework of the argument arc, not only the wording
- The key question early in the process should be: what is the real message the reader should take away, and why is it interesting now?

## 2. Shape the Narrative Before the Prose
- The storyline arc matters as much as sentence-level writing.
- The piece should usually establish:
  - what is happening now
  - why it represents a break, contrast, or strategic shift
  - why the reader should care
- A useful default structure is:
  - short hook
  - concise "in brief" section
  - four to six sections that build the argument
  - close with practical implications
- The goal is not to list ideas. It is to build a memorable executive argument.

## 3. Write in an Executive Style
- The writing should be direct, commercially legible, and easy to absorb.
- Avoid:
  - list-style idea dumping
  - vague or academic framing
  - overly long, repetitive sentences
  - ambiguity that weakens impact
- Prefer:
  - clear sentence openings
  - strong verbs
  - varied sentence structure
  - language that helps the reader act on the argument

## 4. Make the Piece Useful, Not Merely Interesting
- Executive thought leadership should leave the reader with something concrete they can use.
- Each article should answer:
  - what is the strategic point?
  - what does it change?
  - what should a leader do about it now?
- The most valuable articles do not just sound smart. They clarify decisions.

## 5. Treat Research as Part of the Writing System
- Good research should happen both before and after drafting.
- Up front, research the topic deeply enough to shape the argument well.
- After the draft exists, find sources and proof points that corroborate the key claims.
- This strengthens credibility and precision, and reduces the risk that the article reads like unsupported opinion.

## 6. Practical Process Standard
A strong default process is:
1. define the core thesis
2. shape the narrative arc
3. draft the first version
4. critique structure and tone
5. rewrite
6. add targeted evidence where it materially strengthens the piece

The point is not complexity for its own sake. The point is to create a repeatable standard for executive-quality output.
